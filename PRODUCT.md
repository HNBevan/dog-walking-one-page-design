# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Affluent dog owners within about ten miles of Folkestone, Kent (UK), including Hythe, Sandgate, Cheriton, and Capel-le-Ferne - busy professionals, multi-dog households, and owners who treat their dogs as family. They are time-poor, quality-conscious, and choosing a dog walker the way they'd choose any trusted household service: on reliability, insurance, and consistency, not on novelty. They land on this page deciding whether to inquire about a consultation or booking.

## Product Purpose

A one-page marketing site for Hound & Heath, a premium dog walking company serving Kent. It exists to position the business as a serious, professional service (not a casual side-hustle dog walker) and convert visitors into booking inquiries via the contact form. Success is a qualified inquiry from an owner who already trusts the brand before they've spoken to anyone.

## Brand Personality

Bold, architectural, confident, editorial. Premium and restrained rather than cute or playful - this is closer to a luxury hospitality or design-led service brand than a typical pastel pet-care site. The dog is family; the service is run with the seriousness of a property manager or private chef, not a casual dog-walking app.

## Anti-references

Generic AI-template landing pages: centered hero over a soft gradient, Inter/Roboto everywhere, three equal feature cards with icons, pastel "cute pet startup" palettes, stock photo grids of golden retrievers, AI-purple glow buttons, paw-print iconography used decoratively. This should not read as a Wag/Rover clone.

## Design Principles

1. Confidence over cuteness - this is a premium service brand first, a dog brand second. Scale and typography communicate authority.
2. Editorial scale, architectural restraint - massive type and asymmetry signal intent and control, not chaos. Whitespace is structural, not leftover.
3. Trust is structural, not decorative - reliability, insurance, and consistency messaging carry the actual sell; visual boldness earns attention, the copy earns the booking.
4. One bold gesture per section - motion and scale are deployed with a reason each time, never as constant ambient decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text contrast, 3:1 large text). Full keyboard navigation. All motion above static must respect `prefers-reduced-motion` and degrade to instant/crossfade transitions.
